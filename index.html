human baggage

The 'human baggage' series uses collage and mono print, exploring the history of human development that we carry as a society, even though we think that our contemporary civilization is evolved and superior to the barbarities of the past, we continue to commit the same patterns.

We have only scratched the surface of 'human evolution'.

The plastic bag is used in the monotype because in our contemporary times it is the object that humanity has created that represents use and rapid disposal, that novelty of the 1970s, plastic bags are now a ubiquitous product found in every corner of the world. Produced at a rate of up to a trillion bags a year, they have begun to appear at the deepest points of the ocean, at the top of Mount Everest, and even on the polar ice caps, as well as human dispersal around the world.

Bags represent the modern, the new and the disposable, as well as cultural aspects that, when pushed aside by time, become despised.
